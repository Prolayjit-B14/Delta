Dual LED Blinking Circuit

By Ankan Bhowmik

üìÖ Post Date: January 25, 2025

Concept

A dual LED blinker circuit uses transistors as switches to alternate the blinking of two LEDs. The transistors control the current flow to each LED, allowing them to blink in a coordinated manner. This setup is commonly used in signal indicators and decorative lighting.

Components Required

  • Arduino Uno R3
  • 2 x NPN Transistors (BJT)
  • Red LED
  • Blue LED
  • 4 x 1 k‚Ñ¶ Resistor
  • Breadboard
  • Jumper wires
  • Power supply

Connections

  • Connect the Arduino GND pin to the breadboard ground rail.
  • Connect the Arduino 5V pin to the breadboard power rail.
  • Place the red LED on the breadboard. Connect the anode (longer leg) to the collector of the first NPN transistor.
  • Connect the cathode (shorter leg) of the red LED to the ground rail.
  • Connect the emitter of the first NPN transistor to the ground rail.
  • Connect a 1kŒ© resistor between the base of the first NPN transistor and digital pin 6 on the Arduino.
  • Place the blue LED on the breadboard. Connect the anode (longer leg) to the collector of the second NPN transistor.
  • Connect the cathode (shorter leg) of the blue LED to the ground rail.
  • Connect the emitter of the second NPN transistor to the ground rail.
  • Connect a 1kŒ© resistor between the base of the second NPN transistor and digital pin 3 on the Arduino.
  • Connect a 1kŒ© resistor between the 5V rail and the collector of the first NPN transistor.
  • Connect a 1kŒ© resistor between the 5V rail and the collector of the second NPN transistor.

Arduino Code


// C++ code

void setup()
{
    pinMode(6, OUTPUT);
}

void loop()
{
    digitalWrite(6, HIGH);
    delay(1000); // Wait for 1000 millisecond(s)
    digitalWrite(6, LOW);
    delay(1000); // Wait for 1000 millisecond(s)
}

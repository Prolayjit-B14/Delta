Smart Home Automation

By Ankan Bhowmik

üìÖ Post Date: January 19, 2025

Concept

This Smart Home Automation System, developed using Arduino on Tinkercad, automates lighting and fan control based on motion and temperature. The system uses a PIR sensor to detect motion and a TMP36 temperature sensor to monitor ambient temperature, displaying real-time status on an LCD screen. With components like the Arduino Uno R3, SPDT relay module, and a 12V DC motor, it offers a seamless user experience. This project could be further enhanced with IoT integration for remote control and expanded functionality.

  • Arduino Uno R3
  • TMP36 Temperature Sensor
  • PIR Sensor
  • 16x2 LCD Display
  • SPDT Relay Module
  • 12V DC Motor

Connections

  • LCD Display:
    • Connect RS (Register Select) to digital pin 12 on the Arduino.
    • Connect EN (Enable) to digital pin 11 on the Arduino.
    • Connect D4 to digital pin 5 on the Arduino.
    • Connect D5 to digital pin 4 on the Arduino.
    • Connect D6 to digital pin 3 on the Arduino.
    • Connect D7 to digital pin 2 on the Arduino.

    Learn More: LCD Display-by Arghya Roy

  • PIR Sensor:
    • Connect the signal pin to analog pin A0 on the Arduino.
    • Connect the VCC pin to the 5V pin on the Arduino.
    • Connect the GND pin to the ground (GND) pin on the Arduino.
  • TMP36 Temperature Sensor:
    • Connect the signal pin to analog pin A1 on the Arduino.
    • Connect the VCC pin to the 5V pin on the Arduino.
    • Connect the GND pin to the ground (GND) pin on the Arduino.
  • Light Control (via SPDT Relay Module):
    • Connect the relay input pin to digital pin 6 on the Arduino.
    • Connect the relay output to the light circuit.
  • Fan Control (via SPDT Relay Module):
    • Connect the relay input pin to digital pin 10 on the Arduino.
    • Connect the relay output to the 12V DC Motor (fan).
